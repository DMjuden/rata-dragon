The Forge of Fury

Hace dos siglos, el gran herrero enano Durgedgin el Negro y su clan fueron expulsados de su hogar por una horda de orcos y trols feroces. Estos invasores saquearon el salón ancestral de los enanos y mataron a cuantos pudieron encontrar. En plena retirada, Durgedgin lideró a los sobrevivientes del clan en la búsqueda de un nuevo hogar. Tras años de vagar, los enanos encontraron un vasto sistema de cavernas bajo el Diente de Piedra, una colina áspera y boscosa coronada por un peñasco rocoso y desnudo. Allí, Durgedgin y sus seguidores fundaron la fortaleza de Khundrukar.

Hace cerca de un siglo, un miembro del clan de Durgedgin fue capturado por un poderoso clan orco durante una incursión, revelando así la ubicación de la fortaleza oculta. Los líderes orcos organizaron un gran ejército y marcharon hacia Khundrukar. Durante un asedio disputado que se prolongó por meses, los orcos excavaron túneles a través de las defensas enanas. Finalmente, cuando lograron irrumpir, pasaron a todos por la espada y se llevaron enormes cantidades de botín.

En los años que siguieron a la gran batalla, varias criaturas ocuparon la fortaleza, usándola como base para sus incursiones. En otras ocasiones, la caverna permaneció vacía, salvo por los monstruos inconscientes y sedientos de sangre que acechan en lugares como este. Hasta el día de hoy, las leyendas sobre la Venganza de Durgedgin, también conocido como el Herrero de la Guerra, y las extraordinarias hojas forjadas por los iracundos enanos, siguen resonando de vez en cuando en las tierras cercanas al Diente de Piedra.

Adventure Synopsis

La Forja de Furia se encuentra en las ruinas de la fortaleza Khundrukar. Esta aventura está diseñada para cuatro personajes de nivel tres, quienes pueden llegar al nivel cinco en el transcurso de una buena sesión. Los personajes llegan a Diente de Piedra en busca del legendario escondite de las insuperables cuchillas de Durgeddin, solo para descubrir que la fortaleza está infestada de peligrosos monstruos. El complejo se divide en las siguientes cinco secciones:

  • The Mountain Door. La Puerta Montaña constituye el nivel superior del complejo de la caverna. Actualmente, está habitada por una tribu de orcos feroces, liderados por un brutal ogro conocido como @UUID[Actor.NZBQRVqUFg4k45yH]{Great Ulfe}.

  • The Glitterhame. El nivel más grande de Khundrukar, el Glitterhame, es una extensión de hermosas cavernas naturales ahora infestadas de trogloditas y otros monstruos que habitan en cuevas.

  • The Sinkhole. Las corrientes en el Glitterhame descienden hacia un río subterráneo, que recorre una esquina de las cavernas olvidada por los habitantes de arriba.

  • The Foundry. Más allá del Glitterhame se encuentra un complejo de cámaras y pasadizos tallados por el clan de Durgedgin. Un pequeño grupo de duergar (enanos grises) actualmente controla el salón de Durgedgin, trabajando para descubrir los secretos de la antigua forja del herrero. Una gran grieta desciende hacia el Lago Negro.

  • The Black Lake. El habitante más peligroso de las cavernas bajo el Diente de Piedra mora en las frías y tranquilas aguas del Lago Negro. Nightscale, una joven dragón negro, descubrió un pasaje hacia el lago a través de un sifón subacuático conectado a una laguna en el otro lado de la colina. El dragón ha reclamado las antiguas riquezas de Khundrukar como su tesoro.

ABOUT THE ORIGINAL

The Forge of Fury, by Richard Baker, was originally published in 2000 as an adventure for the third edition of the D&D game.

The adventure was published shortly after The Sunless Citadel and was designed as a follow-up to it. Characters who succeeded in that mission and advanced to 3rd level were now ready to take on the challenges of a ruined dwarven fortress.

Character Hooks

Podés ubicar el Diente de Piedra y sus cavernas en cualquier lugar del mundo de tu campaña. La Forja de Furia encaja bien en una zona de colinas ásperas o tierras altas, a varios días de distancia de las comunidades más cercanas. El pueblo de Blasingdell está brevemente descrito en esta aventura, por si necesitás un lugar donde los personajes puedan hacer los preparativos finales.

Cualquier escenario que incluya una región montañosa tiene una ubicación lista para usar para la fortaleza enana en La Forja de Furia.

Dragonlance: En Krynn, Khundrukar puede situarse en cualquier lugar de las Montañas Kharolis. Como una fortaleza independiente sin túneles directos que la conecten con Thorbardin, fue invadida por atacantes durante la Edad de la Fortaleza. En la Edad de la Desesperación, fue tomada por las fuerzas de Verminaard. Considerá reemplazar a los orcos en la aventura con hobgoblins y a los duergar con enanos Theiwar.

Eberron: Antiguamente un puesto avanzado en las Mror Holds occidentales, Khundrukar fue sitiada y limpiada de sus habitantes cuando el Reino de Galifar invadió esa tierra. Los agresores dejaron el lugar desocupado tras su victoria. Desde entonces, ha caído en manos de varios grupos de monstruos.

Forgotten Realms: Situado al noroeste de Mirabar en la Espina del Mundo, Khundrukar era una fortaleza de enanos escudo que fue invadida por orcos renegados de la tribu Many-Arrows, descontentos con lo que veían como la traición de su reino a las viejas costumbres.

Greyhawk: Khundrukar se encuentra en el Pomarj, en las colinas Drachensgrab occidentales. La fortaleza cayó poco después de las Guerras del Odio, cuando una oleada de orcos y otros invasores humanoides malignos arrasaron la región.

Wilderness Encounters and Camping

If you want to make the trek from Blasingdell to the Stone Tooth more interesting, you can create random encounters with orc raiding parties (see "Wait and Watch" below) or with creatures native to forest or hills. See appendix B of the Dungeon Master's Guide for lists of monsters by environment.

Wait and Watch

Los personajes pueden optar por esperar y observar el camino antes de enfrentarse al Diente de Piedra. Al llegar, dos grupos de forrajeadores de la tribu de @UUID[Actor.NZBQRVqUFg4k45yH]{Great Ulfe} están buscando riquezas en las colinas cercanas. El primer grupo, compuesto por cuatro @UUID[Actor.25wBudxOWVqYc2Fc]{orcos}, regresa dos días después de la llegada de los personajes, y el segundo grupo tres días después del primero. Estos orcos duermen de día y viajan de noche, por lo que llegan a las inmediaciones del grupo antes del amanecer.

Si el grupo establece un campamento a al menos 90 pies del camino y no realiza ninguna acción que llame la atención, los orcos que regresan pasarán sin detectar el campamento. De lo contrario, los orcos notarán el campamento cuando estén a 90 pies de distancia (o el doble si el campamento tiene una fogata encendida o el grupo no está tratando de ser sigiloso). Los personajes que monten guardia y tengan una Percepción pasiva de 10 o más, detectarán a los orcos acercándose.

Los orcos no suelen notar a un guardia escondido en el bosque; compara su Percepción pasiva con una tirada de Destreza (Sigilo) del personaje.

Cuatro humanoides encorvados y cubiertos con sucias armaduras de cuero avanzan por el camino, gruñendo y murmurando entre ellos en una lengua gutural. Los colmillos amarillos sobresalen de sus rostros bestiales, dándoles una apariencia aún más feroz y salvaje.

Si los orcos llegan al campamento y no son descubiertos, tratarán de eliminar a los que estén de guardia o escabullirse en el campamento para atacar a los personajes mientras duermen.

Si un grupo de orcos pasa el campamento sin ser enfrentado o lucha para llegar al otro lado, los sobrevivientes reforzarán las defensas en el complejo (ver @UUID[JournalEntry.2OpW5YlA40pEr39f.JournalEntryPage.0000000000000001#5.-orc-cave]{area 5} and @UUID[JournalEntry.2OpW5YlA40pEr39f.JournalEntryPage.0000000000000001#14.-bunk-room]{area 14}).

Los orcos capturados pueden describir con algunos detalles la organización general de la Puerta Montaña. No saben nada sobre el resto del complejo ni que se puede entrar por la chimenea. Los orcos que defienden la Puerta Montaña se niegan a negociar por el rescate.

Los personajes podrían disfrazarse como orcos utilizando equipo capturado. Esto podría permitirles engañar a los centinelas y entrar al calabozo, utilizando una tirada de Carisma (Engaño) enfrentada contra una tirada de Sabiduría (Intuición) de los orcos.

Khundrukar: General Features

p36

A menos que se indique lo contrario en la descripción de una área particular, los siguientes aspectos son ciertos en Khundrukar:

Puertas:

La mayoría de las puertas están hechas de madera o piedra. Los personajes pueden intentar varias acciones antes de abrir una puerta, como escuchar los sonidos del otro lado y comprobar si hay trampas. Si los personajes deciden escuchar en una puerta, revisa la descripción del cuarto más allá para determinar si hay criaturas en esa área. Si no se proporcionan instrucciones específicas, un personaje que escuche en la puerta y tenga éxito en una tirada de Sabiduría (Percepción) DC 10 puede oír sonidos de actividad; aumenta la DC a 15 para sonidos más suaves.

Luz:

Dentro de los límites de Khundrukar, todas las áreas están oscuras a menos que la descripción de la sala diga lo contrario.

Ventilación: Todas las áreas señaladas contienen un suministro adecuado de aire. El aire se renueva a través de ventilaciones que conducen a la superficie. Estas ventilaciones son individualmente demasiado pequeñas para que las criaturas, excepto las muy pequeñas, puedan navegar por ellas.

The Mountain Door

El nivel superior de Khundrukar, conocido como la Puerta Montaña, es una caverna natural que fue ampliada y mejorada por el clan de Durgedgin, quienes crearon una serie de salas y cámaras de guardia para proteger la entrada a su reino. El lugar sigue estando bien fortificado, incluso hoy en día.

Este antiguo bastión enano ahora está ocupado por una banda de saqueadores orcos, liderados por un feroz ogro que se hace llamar @UUID[Actor.NZBQRVqUFg4k45yH]{Gran Ulfe}. Estos saqueadores emergen de la Puerta Montaña para cazar y saquear en los alrededores. Para mantener su guarida segura, los orcos han bloqueado el acceso a los niveles inferiores de Khundrukar, y rara vez se encuentran con otros habitantes del sistema de cavernas.

At the west end of the cavern, the ceiling lowers to meet the surface of the river. The dwarves of Khundrukar once resided in private rooms to the north of the great hall. When the orcs came through, they cut down all the dwarves they found and stripped the treasure out of the place.

The door opens into a large chamber with a dark pool in the center. Bits of smashed furniture and other debris are scattered about the floor. Hallways lead off to the east and the west.

A weird moaning fills the area, slowly becoming recognizable as Dwarvish curses. The sound rises to angry shouting and grows closer. Then the translucent form of an armored dwarf appears through a wall and attacks!

Creature.

Area 43, @UUID[JournalEntry.2OpW5YlA40pEr39f.JournalEntryPage.0000000000000001#46.-common-area]{area 46}, and all the rooms marked 44 are now the demesne of the dwarf mage Arundil, who lingers as an insane @UUID[Actor.ns1daWcTampUxyeV]{ghost}.

The ghost relentlessly attacks anyone who enters its territory (most likely in either area 43 or area 46) but can't pursue characters who retreat beyond its domain. If the ghost is turned, it flees to one of the empty rooms labeled as area 44.

Development.

Arundil's ghost is tormented by grief and shame over abandoning his kin to die (see @UUID[JournalEntry.2OpW5YlA40pEr39f.JournalEntryPage.0000000000000001#47.-arundils-chambers]{area 47}). The spirit might be laid to rest if the characters can convince it that they have cleansed Khundrukar.

44. Looted Rooms

Severak chambers in the Foundry were once living quarters, drawing rooms, barracks, armories, storerooms, and so on. Now they contain only smashed furniture and debris from the final battle, graffiti in the Orc language, and skeletal remains of dwarves and orcs.

Treasure.

Here and there a remnant of something valuable can be salvaged from the trash. Roll a [[/r d10]] the first time a character searches a given room; on a result of 10, the character uncovers a semiprecious stone (see the @UUID[RollTable.6JdJONYdaC67TEeV]{10 gp Gemstones table} in chapter 7 of the Dungeon Master's Guide).

45. Skeleton Room

One of the smaller chambers once served as a small barracks. Half a dozen dwarves perished here and were later animated by Arundil (see @UUID[JournalEntry.2OpW5YlA40pEr39f.JournalEntryPage.0000000000000001#47.-arundils-chambers]{area 47}). He ordered them to defend their post against all intruders.

This chamber might once have been a barracks. The remains of six wooden bunks lean against the walls, burned and hacked by pillagers, and half a dozen skeletal dwarves lie here and there on the floor. With clicking and scraping sounds, the skeletons rise and advance on you.

Creatures.

The six @UUID[Actor.pdpm8ppcOcACHkUm]{skeletons} attack any non-undead creature that enters. They don't pursue anyone beyond this chamber.

Treasure.

The rogue from the failed expedition lies in this room, her desiccated corpse half-buried under wrecked furniture. A pouch on her belt contains 670 sp.

46. Common Area

The open courtyard in the east end of the residential area was the site of a pitched battle between dwarves and orcs.

The hallway leads to a large open chamber. Three doors open into it, and a dark pool fills a low stone basin in its center. The chamber is littered with the remains of old warriors. Seven dwarf corpses lie where they fell a hundred years ago, surrounded by the remains of at least a dozen orc warriors. The dead have been stripped of their arms and armor; only a handful of broken weapons and shattered shields remain.

Creature.

If the characters approach this area from the secret door to the south before entering @UUID[JournalEntry.2OpW5YlA40pEr39f.JournalEntryPage.0000000000000001#43.-entrance-to-the-dwarf-halls]{area 43}, Arundil the @UUID[Actor.ns1daWcTampUxyeV]{ghost} confronts them here; refer to the encounter in area 43. Otherwise, there is nothing dangerous or valuable in the area.

47. Arundil's Chambers

Arundil, the leading mage of Khundrukar and Durgeddin's trusted advisor, lived in a spacious room close to the great hall. During the final assault of the orcs, Arundil used his magic to escape the slaughter. He returned to find all his kinfolk dead. Driven insane with grief, he tried to repair some of the complex's defenses, animating dead warriors and various objects to kill any intruders that returned. In his madness, he even summoned a fiend (see @UUID[JournalEntry.2OpW5YlA40pEr39f.JournalEntryPage.0000000000000001#49.-idallas-den]{area 49}), and he perished at its hands not long after.

This chamber must once have been the personal quarters of an important dwarf. It is decorated with tapestries and furnished comfortably, although age has taken its toll on the contents. Apparently, the room has not been looted. A beautiful rug covers the center of the floor, and a simple bed and writing desk stand in the southwest corner.

Creature.

The rug, 20 feet square, is actually a Gargantuan @UUID[Actor.uFjM6pZVjjT2OpVg]{rug of smothering} that activates when any non-dwarf sets foot on it. It fights until destroyed but doesn't attack a dwarf under any circumstances. See "@UUID[JournalEntry.2OpW5YlA40pEr39f.JournalEntryPage.0000000000000001#arundils-animated-objects]{Arundil's Animated Objects}" above.

Treasure.

Beneath the bed is a small wooden chest containing 1,200 sp, 3,000 cp, and a square of silk in which are wrapped a star sapphire worth 500 gp and a pair of matched blue topazes worth 90 gp each.

48. Looted Armory

Khundrukar's armory was looted long ago. Its door is made of iron plates, but the lock was broken during the pillaging—it can be forced open with a successful DC 15 Strength check.

Rows of empty weapon racks and a dozen or more armor stands indicate that this room was once an armory. Some debris litters the floor, but nothing of value is readily apparent.

Creature.

One of the armor stands is actually an animated object (use the @UUID[Actor.uw9hwfBtGoWftIz8]{animated armor} stat block), which activates when a non-dwarf enters the room. It fights until destroyed. See "@UUID[JournalEntry.2OpW5YlA40pEr39f.JournalEntryPage.0000000000000001#arundils-animated-objects]{Arundil's Animated Objects}" above.

49. Idalla's Den

The mad dwarf Arundil used every magical tool at his disposal to protect the complex. He not only animated objects and the bones of the dead but called upon more dangerous magic in his futile attempt to make the place safe. In the former chambers of a dwarf scholar, tucked away in the eastern end of Khundrukar, he found a scroll that he tried to use to bind a fiend to his service.

This room was once a library or a study. Bookshelves stand against the walls. Piles of books rest on a reading table in the middle of the room. The air reeks of moldy paper. Doors exit to the north, east, and south.

A beautiful, slender woman with long black hair sits in a chair at the table, looking extremely sad. Her clothing, though well worn, does little to detract from her appearance.

Creature.

Arundil summoned a @UUID[Actor.25zepv0kfN4Wr50u]{succubus} and commanded it to guard the area that is now the domain of Arundil's ghost (see @UUID[JournalEntry.2OpW5YlA40pEr39f.JournalEntryPage.0000000000000001#43.-entrance-to-the-dwarf-halls]{area 43}). But the fiend easily overpowered Arundil's will, then toyed with him cruelly until it finally drained his life force.

The succubus, which calls itself Idalla, claimed the scholar's library for its own and spends much time here reading through the collected lore. It learned of Khundrukar's history from the scholar's records and realized that the legends of wondrous treasure would lure greedy souls. It consumed the wizard from the previous expedition, who took refuge in this library after his companions were killed. Since that time, it has spread rumors to draw more adventurers to its lair.

Idalla takes the form of a human woman. When the characters enter, the fiend jumps up and begs to be released from imprisonment. Idalla tells the characters that she is a captive of a wizard who resides below this level. The succubus plays the role to the hilt, trembling and crying as it spins a tale of woe. It tries to split up the group, persuading one character to remain (using its Charm attack if necessary) while the others deal with the wizard. If they leave anyone alone with Idalla, the fiend attempts to kiss and drain the hapless dupe. The succubus is an adept liar and mixes in just enough of the truth to be convincing; see the "Idalla's Story" sidebar.

If the characters attack, Idalla attempts to charm the most dangerous-looking character and turn him or her against the party while the fiend fades into the Ethereal.

Idalla's Story

p57

Idalla takes delight in misleading the characters. The fiend can impart the following bits of information:

  • The wizard lives below this area. He wants to use Idalla for a study on prolonging life. (False.)
  • The wizard told Idalla a bit of the history of Khundrukar and the fate of Durgeddin, which she can pass on. (The information is true, but the succubus learned history from reading the books here, not from the wizard.)
  • A doppelganger lurks in the Glitterhame. (False.)
  • A dead dwarf haunts the halls, reduced to a mad, gibbering spirit. (True; this is the ghost of Arundil.)
  • An ancient dragon lairs below the Foundry. (Partly true; Nightscale is a young dragon.)

49a (Bedchamber).

The adjoining room was the sleeping quarters of the dwarf scholar. It is moldy and very stuffy. Anyone who looks around the room finds a secret compartment in one of the walls with a successful DC 15 Wisdom (&Reference[skill=Perception]) check.

Treasure.

The dwarf scholar amassed a small amount of treasure, which he kept in the secret compartment: 110 gp and a cat's-eye gem worth 70 gp. In the library, in addition to an extensive collection of dwarven lore, the characters can find four @UUID[Item.UuyDCacPahzTNdZo]{spell scrolls} (of @UUID[Item.pGZ5m5lChUOWdLHP]{alarm}, @UUID[Item.POCRRDWNqqVabtXx]{disguise self}, @UUID[Item.DlFlvxewI37T7Nn2]{enlarge/reduce}, and @UUID[Item.Bq22tltxKTjyj8uR]{shield}). The shelves might also include more scrolls, useful information tying in to future adventures, items of value to a collector, or other interesting lore, as you see fit.

The Black Lake

The darkest and most remote corner of the Glitterhame's cavern system, the Black Lake is a large, winding cavern filled with water that drains from the Dark Mere through a submerged siphon. From the lake, the subterranean river flows west through the Sinkhole, across the bottom of the chasm in @UUID[JournalEntry.2OpW5YlA40pEr39f.JournalEntryPage.0000000000000001#3.-the-rift-hall]{area 3} of the Mountain Door, and onward for about 5 miles until it emerges at the surface.

The dwarves of Khundrukar never settled these caverns, although Durgeddin had his followers begin work on an escape route through the depths.

The Black Lake is now the domain of Nightscale, a @UUID[Actor.lJPjgMU0v792ptgE]{young black dragon}. Here she has built her hoard, having slowly collected most of the remaining loot from Khundrukar. From the lake, Nightscale can swim to the Sinkhole, climb up to the Glitterhame, or fly up to the Foundry through the chasm there. The troglodytes fear and worship the dragon. The duergar have negotiated an uneasy truce with Nightscale, paying her a modest tribute to leave them alone.

51. Dwarven Bridges

Durgeddin's followers built two stone bridges when they started work on the escape route through this level. The chain ladder that drops from the north ledge of area 38 ends just above the ground in the area south of the western bridge.

If the characters reached this area by using the chain ladder or otherwise descending through the chasm, read or paraphrase the following text. Adjust the information as needed if they arrive here by a different route.

A fast-moving underground river roars from east to west under a stone bridge. The bottom of the chain ladder is near the south bank of the river. On the north side of the bridge, a ledge continues to the east.

If the characters go north and cross the bridge, read:

After a short distance, the ledge leading east bends to the south, and a second bridge surmounts the river.

The western bridge is safe, but the bridge to the east is not. It has settled considerably, some of its stone blocks leaning precariously. Also, the stone has a curiously pitted appearance (which was caused by Nightscale's acid breath). A character who succeeds on a DC 10 Intelligence (&Reference[skill=Investigation]) check can determine that the stone was affected by acid, although the source of the acid isn't apparent.

Hazard.

The weakened bridge can't take much weight before it collapses. If more than one Small or larger creature is on the bridge at one time, or if a character in heavy armor steps onto it, the arch fails. Any creature on the span must succeed on a DC 10 Dexterity saving throw to catch itself on the ledge. On a failed save, a creature falls, takes 7 ([[/r 2d6]]) bludgeoning damage from falling timbers and stones, and is caught in the river's current (see "@UUID[JournalEntry.2OpW5YlA40pEr39f.JournalEntryPage.0000000000000001#fast-flowing-water]{Fast-Flowing Water}" above).

52. Nightscale's Lair

When the characters first approach the southeastern shore of the lake, Nightscale is dozing on her hoard on the island (area 53). The dragon is immediately alerted to their presence if the characters make noise, carry light sources, or trigger the collapse of the bridge. Otherwise, the characters don't draw the dragon's attention unless they move all the way to the ledge directly south of area 53.

The dilapidated bridge leads to another ledge that winds southeast alongside a very large lake, apparently the source of the underground river. The cavern is quite large; the lake's far shore is lost in shadow, and the echoes of water seem to indicate a very sizable cave.

Creature.

When Nightscale becomes aware of intruders, the @UUID[Actor.lJPjgMU0v792ptgE]{young black dragon} slips into the water, quietly swimming to a point near the three stepping stones shown on the map. Any character watching the lake can make a Wisdom (&Reference[skill=Perception]) check contested by the dragon's Dexterity (&Reference[skill=Stealth]) check. On a successful check, the character catches a glimpse of a serpentine form moving in the darkness.

The dragon takes full advantage of her aquatic environment and never offers an easy target to enemies. She begins combat at a range of 20 to 30 feet, raising only her head and part of her neck above the water (granting her &Reference[rule=Cover]{three-quarters cover}) and breathing acid. Then she submerges and waits for her breath weapon to recharge. Nightscale doesn't resort to melee combat unless she is confronted underwater. If the dragon is reduced to 40 hit points or fewer, she tries to escape through the passage in @UUID[JournalEntry.2OpW5YlA40pEr39f.JournalEntryPage.0000000000000001#54.-the-dragons-passage]{area 54}.

Negotiation.

Nightscale isn't inclined to parley with the characters unless they surrender, give her all of their treasure, and throw their arms and armor in the lake. If the characters seriously injure her, Nightscale might bargain to buy time so she can later move her hoard. Only when she is near death does she abandon her treasure.

53. Nightscale's Hoard

When the characters come into view of the island for the first time, read:

A short distance offshore sits a small isle thirty or forty feet across. Stalagmites thrust up at the ceiling above, and the dark waters lap quietly at its shores. Several spots on its surface glimmer even in the gloom, suggesting the promise of treasure to be had.

Nightscale keeps her treasure on the small island in the lake, and she spends most of her time resting here. If the characters have avoided attracting her attention so far and want to approach the island, they can do so quietly if every character's Dexterity (&Reference[skill=Stealth]) check meets or exceeds the dragon's passive Perception score.

Treasure.

Nightscale has accumulated considerable wealth from her plunder of Khundrukar. Her hoard contains 6,200 sp, 1,430 gp, two garnets worth 20 gp each, a black pearl worth 50 gp, a @UUID[Item.NUJ3nZvqkzSzxQFF]{wand of magic missiles}, a @UUID[Item.7In7mBhbBbYWMY2f]{+2 greataxe} bearing Durgeddin's smith-mark, a @UUID[Item.6jtul4akKsjTVjeh]{+1 shield}, a @UUID[Item.5s3iusDKk9uaY1e0]{potion of healing}, and a @UUID[Item.1XAyp62o1ltjdnTT]{potion of flying}.

54. The Dragon's Passage

Beneath the surface of a small pool is an underwater passage that links the Black Lake to the Dark Mere on the east side of the Stone Tooth. The submerged passage is about 700 feet long.

Aftermath

The characters have overcome Khundrukar's dangerous inhabitants and looted the dragon's lair. What next?

Resolving the Character Hooks

If the characters return to Blasingdell, their fame as adventurers spreads. Townsfolk hail them on the street, and some might approach them with problems that can lead to further adventures. The characters can continue to use the town as a base of operations. Other developments depend on what got them here and how they leave.

Follow the Map

While the characters are exploring the stronghold, they might find another map, perhaps on the body of an adventurer from the previous expedition, or in the library in @UUID[JournalEntry.2OpW5YlA40pEr39f.JournalEntryPage.0000000000000001#49.-idallas-den]{area 49}. It could lead to a different dungeon, a city in some land far away, or an as-yet-unknown location rumored to hide a fabulous treasure.

Track the Broken Blade

If the characters were sent to retrieve weapons for Baron Althon, they can bring him the @UUID[Item.FWkMJ8bwFEjaFviK]{+1 longsword} from @UUID[JournalEntry.2OpW5YlA40pEr39f.JournalEntryPage.0000000000000001#22.-fungus-cavern]{area 22}, the @UUID[Item.7In7mBhbBbYWMY2f]{+2 greataxe} in @UUID[JournalEntry.2OpW5YlA40pEr39f.JournalEntryPage.0000000000000001#53.-nightscales-hoard]{area 53}, the @UUID[Item.eeqIcDaC2qduKgvi]{handaxe} from @UUID[JournalEntry.2OpW5YlA40pEr39f.JournalEntryPage.0000000000000001#14.-bunk-room]{area 14}, and the @UUID[Item.OYa1ipmFi8sR52ij]{warhammer} from @UUID[JournalEntry.2OpW5YlA40pEr39f.JournalEntryPage.0000000000000001#23.-dwarven-sepulchers]{area 23}. All bear Durgeddin's smith-mark; Baron Althon pays 500 gp above the market price for each mundane weapon, 1,000 gp for the @UUID[Item.FWkMJ8bwFEjaFviK]{+1 longsword}, and 6,000 gp for the @UUID[Item.7In7mBhbBbYWMY2f]{+2 greataxe}, if the characters are willing to sell.

Vanquish the Orc Raiders

The characters can return to Blasingdell at any time to collect the bounty for orcs they kill or capture. If the characters eliminate all the orcs in the stronghold, the mayor honors them with a celebration attended by the eminent citizens of the town. The characters have established valuable contacts who can aid them in their later endeavors.

Further Adventures

You can build an entire campaign around the duergar that occupy the Foundry. Perhaps they came here from a hidden city in even deeper caverns beneath the Sinkhole and the Black Lake. Your answers to the following questions form the basis for further adventures:

  • How did the duergar get into the complex?
  • What is the mysterious project they are working on?
  • Did Durgeddin know of the duergar's existence?
  • Do the duergar have some of Durgeddin's items? If so, are they guarding them or using them?

You can also use this adventure to sow the seeds of the characters' next activity. Perhaps the party encounters a legend of a magical treasure from Khundrukar's fall, buried in the tomb of an evil knight. Maybe @UUID[Actor.NZBQRVqUFg4k45yH]{Great Ulfe}'s ogre clan is terrorizing a nearby area, or an orc war chief wields a legendary weapon that was crafted by Durgeddin.

Whatever form they take, the repercussions of the characters' descent into Khundrukar have the potential to affect their lives for years to come.
